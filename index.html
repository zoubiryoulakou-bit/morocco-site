Music
Jun 19–22
📍 Essaouira

Gnaoua World Music Festival

Cultural Root Gnawa is a UNESCO-recognized spiritual practice of West African origin — a living ritual of healing, trance, and community kept alive by Moroccan masters.

Four days of Gnawa maalem fusing with jazz, blues, and global artists on Essaouira's magical Atlantic stages. Free outdoor concerts, oceanic sunsets, soulful nights.

Spiritual
May 23–31
📍 Fes

Festival of World Sacred Music

Cultural Root Founded in 1994 as a message of peace, this National Geographic top-25 festival unites sacred music from all world religions inside a 1,200-year-old UNESCO medina.

Sufi chanting, gospel, Indian ragas, and Andalusian malhoun — spiritual melodies bouncing off ancient Fassi walls, connecting every soul in attendance.

Get Tickets
Cinema
Nov 28 – Dec 7
📍 Marrakech

Marrakech International Film Festival

Cultural Root Founded 2001 under royal patronage, MIFF champions African and Arab cinema on the world stage — awarding the prestigious Étoile d'Or from Djemaa el-Fna.

International stars, open-air premieres, and masterclasses with cinema legends — all framed by the magic of the Pink City's ancient rooftops and souks.

Get Tickets
Adventure
Oct 3–5
📍 Merzouga, Sahara

Merzouga Desert Music Festival

Cultural Root Born of Amazigh tradition — gathering around fire, music, and sky. The Saharan people's ancient bond with their vast, sacred landscape, celebrated in sound.

Camel treks at dawn, campfire sessions with master musicians, concerts under a billion Saharan stars. The most extraordinary venue on the face of the Earth.

Get Tickets
Cultural
May 9–11
📍 Kalaat M'Gouna

M'Gouna Rose Festival

Cultural Root The Damask rose arrived from Persia in the 10th century. Today the Valley of Roses produces 4,000+ tonnes annually — each harvest a community celebration of life and land.

Pink petals carpeting mountain roads, folk music in every alley, parades honoring the Rose Queen. The warmest, most fragrant community gathering in Morocco.

Get Tickets
Jazz
Apr 11–13
📍 Casablanca

Jazzablanca Festival

Cultural Root Jazz arrived in Casablanca via American GIs in WWII and took root in the city's cosmopolitan soul. Since 2006, Jazzablanca has been the rhythmic heartbeat of Africa's most international city.

World-class headliners, local jazz brilliance, and legendary rooftop après-parties on the Atlantic coast. Casablanca at its most electric.
